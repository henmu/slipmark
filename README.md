# Slipmark

A browser extension for saving, organizing, and tracking anything you discover online.

Unlike bookmarks, Slipmark is designed around entries and labels rather than folders. Every saved item can belong to multiple categories, making it easy to organize media, websites, articles, games, books, videos, and more.

## Features

- Multi-label organization
- Primary labels (Movie, Manga, Game, etc.)
- Custom labels
- Rich metadata
- Powerful filtering
- Fast search
- Import and export
- Firefox support
- Chrome support (planned)

## Why?

Traditional browser bookmarks treat a URL as the thing you're saving. That works well until the page changes, the service shuts down, the content moves, or you simply want to remember more than just where you found it.

Slipmark takes a different approach.

Instead of making the URL the bookmark, Slipmark makes the item the bookmark. Whether it's a TV show, manga, novel, recipe, game, article, video, or website, the entry represents the thing itself—not a specific webpage.

URLs become editable metadata attached to an entry. If content moves to another website or streaming service, you don't lose your notes, progress, labels, or organization. You simply update or add a new URL while keeping the same entry.

For example, if you're halfway through a TV series and it leaves one streaming service, you can keep your progress, add the new streaming link, and continue without creating a new bookmark or losing track of where you were.

Slipmark is designed to be a personal catalog of the things that matter to you, rather than a collection of links that may or may not exist in the future.
